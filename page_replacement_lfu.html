How LFU Works

Concept

The Least Frequently Used (LFU) page replacement algorithm replaces the page that has been used the least number of times.

It's based on the idea that pages with low usage frequency are less important than frequently used pages.

Implementation

LFU can be implemented using:

  • A frequency counter for each page
  • A min-heap to track the least frequently used page
  • Time of last use to break ties

When a page needs to be replaced, the page with the smallest frequency count is selected.

Advantages

  • Effective for workloads with stable access patterns
  • Good at preserving frequently used pages
  • Performs well in certain specialized applications
  • Better than FIFO for some access patterns

Disadvantages

  • Higher overhead than FIFO or LRU
  • May retain obsolete pages with high initial frequency
  • Poor performance with rapidly changing access patterns
  • Susceptible to Belady's Anomaly
